Twisted Minesweeper

Version 0.1.2 (unstable, expect bugs)

Click the grid to start the game.

HOW TO PLAY

Twisted Minesweeper is based on standard minesweeper, but with a couple of additions and modifications.

The Grid

There is a grid (as you saw), and your goal is to reveal all the squares on the board EXCEPT the mines. The amount of mines is the same amount as the flags you have, and the amount of fake mines is half of the amount of mines. There are 3 "magic tiles".

Revealing Squares

If you reveal a square that is a mine, you lose. When you reveal a square that isn't a mine, it'll show the amount of mines adjacent to it (including diagonal), i.e. the square will display the amount of mines are next to it (max 8, since there can only be 8 squares next to it, diagonals count). Note that it also counts the FAKE mines, so be careful! If there are no mines next to it, it'll display a complex number (e.g. 3+5i, -2+3i, 6-2i, etc.) which represents the relative position of either a random square that is next to a fake mine or the fake mine itself. The real part of the number represents horizontal translation and the imaginary part (the number that is suffixed with 'i') represents vertical translation. For example, if a square reveals "3i+5" then it means that the square 3 right and 5 up of that square is either where the fake mine is, or where the fake mine is next to. Fake mines do not make you lose when you reveal it.

Magic Tiles

There are also "magic tiles" that shift the mines around when you click on it.

Flags

You can flag a tile if you think that a mine is there so that you can keep track of how many mines are left and where they are. However, flagging is optional.

Controls

Left click on a square on the board to reveal it.

Right click on a square on the board to flag it.

Have fun!
